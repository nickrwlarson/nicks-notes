Calgary, Alberta, Canada

In my day job, I'm a Director of Product Marketing at a large software company. But I have several other jobs. The most important is being a father. Another is being a lifelong learner.

I've graduated from university four times, read hundreds of books, and completed more professional certifications than I can easily remember. But that's not really the point. I simply enjoy learning — whether that's cooking a better meal, getting more confident on my snowboard, or figuring out how to wire a light switch without burning the house down.

For the last decade, I've been refining how I read. Not just consuming as many books as possible, but trying to turn their ideas into lessons I can actually use.

I highlight heavily, leave tabs, and take copious notes on my iPad. Over time, I started sharing these notes with friends and colleagues, who found them helpful for discovering new ideas and deciding what to read next.

Every post here is primarily for me. Writing is how I think. Publishing simply forces me to sharpen those thoughts and hold myself accountable.

If it helps you discover a great book, decide one isn't worth your time, or pick up an interesting idea along the way, that's a bonus.
